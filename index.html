About

I study how AI systems behave when labels are noisy, signals are multimodal, and evaluation matters.

My research spans sample difficulty, noisy supervision, fairness, missingness, synthetic data, medical imaging, automated pain monitoring, VLM evaluation, and agentic AI. The common thread is reliability: models should be evaluated in ways that reflect the real task, not just the easiest benchmark.

Health AI appears throughout my work because it is a rigorous setting for ML research: labels can be uncertain, rare cases matter, data are multimodal, and deployment requires care. I try to bring that same standard to every AI system I work on.
