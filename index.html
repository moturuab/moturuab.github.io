About

A research background with clinical, product, teaching, and governance experience.

I am a Computer Science PhD candidate at the University of Toronto, supervised by Dr. Anna Goldenberg and Dr. Babak Taati. I have worked across core ML methods, medical imaging, health AI evaluation, and AI education with the Vector Institute, SickKids, UHN KITE, T-CAIREM, and CoordCare.

01

Noisy labels and sample difficulty

Lightweight methods for learning which samples are difficult, using that signal to improve noisy training, fairness, label correction, and synthetic data use.

02

Medical computer vision

Clinical imaging work across pediatric whole-body MRI, renal ultrasound, lung cancer detection, synthetic imaging, and facial pain monitoring.

03

VLMs and agentic AI

Current work includes vision-language reasoning, RL for medical VLMs, and healthcare prototypes that combine AI workflows with clinical constraints.

04

AI translation

I teach and organize AI in medicine sessions for technical and clinical audiences, including medical residents, trainees, and interdisciplinary groups.
