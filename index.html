About

My work starts with a simple question: which examples should a model trust?

I develop methods that account for differences in sample difficulty rather than treating every training example as equally reliable. This includes work on sample weighting, label correction, fairness, missingness, and targeted synthetic data.

In parallel, I work with clinicians on pediatric whole-body MRI, renal ultrasound, and automated pain assessment. These projects have also led me to study how people use AI tools and how hospitals should evaluate, teach, and govern them.

Recent papers and a few earlier projects that shaped my research.

LiBaGS: Lightweight Boundary Gap Synthesis

A generator-agnostic method for selecting synthetic examples that fill sparse but useful regions near a decision boundary.

2026

When Does RL Help Medical VLMs?

A controlled study of what vision, supervised fine-tuning, and reinforcement learning each contribute to medical VLM performance.

2026

Moving Beyond One-Size-Fits-All AI Education

An argument for tailoring healthcare AI education to the model, intended user, clinical specialty, condition, and setting.

LiLAW: Lightweight Learnable Adaptive Weighting

A three-parameter approach that learns how much weight to give easy, moderate, and hard samples during training.
