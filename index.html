About

A research profile with clinical, product, and teaching context.

My work sits at the point where machine learning research meets clinical reality. I care about models that are not only accurate in a paper, but robust to noisy labels, shifts in sample difficulty, missing time-series data, synthetic data choices, and the constraints of human review.

I have worked with radiologists, long-term care teams, medical educators, and AI researchers across UofT, Vector, SickKids, UHN KITE, T-CAIREM, and Massey College. That has made me comfortable moving between theory, implementation, evaluation, communication, and governance.

What I am best positioned to do

Build, evaluate, and explain ML systems for settings where bad labels, missing data, subgroup differences, and human decisions affect model quality.

  • Noisy-label learning and per-sample weighting
  • Medical computer vision and multimodal health AI
  • Model evaluation with clinicians and real deployment constraints

Why my background is different

I have paired core ML research with clinical collaborations, long-term care deployment support, AI curriculum work, reviewing, teaching, and startup technical leadership.

Roles I am targeting

Research Scientist, Applied Scientist, ML Engineer, AI Research Engineer, and early technical leadership roles where research judgment and product sense both matter.

Recent work, in plain English.

Noisy learning

Sample difficulty as a practical signal

I am developing lightweight methods that estimate which samples are difficult, noisy, or less trustworthy, then use that signal for weighting, label correction, fairness, synthetic data selection, and time-series missingness.

Medical imaging

AI-assisted pediatric imaging and volume-level evaluation

At SickKids and Vector, I worked on generative, classification, and segmentation models for pediatric whole-body MRI and renal ultrasound, including evaluation with radiologists and a NeurIPS workshop spotlight on why patch-level results do not automatically translate to full-volume performance.

3D MRIRenal ultrasoundGANsRadiologist review
Computer vision

Pain monitoring in older adults

At UHN KITE, I worked on automated facial pain detection for older adults with dementia, including pairwise/contrastive learning, synthetic faces, real-time evaluation, and support for long-term care installations.
