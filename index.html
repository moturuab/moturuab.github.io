About

I study how sample difficulty can make machine learning more reliable.

My PhD research develops lightweight methods that learn from differences between training samples. LiLAW adapts the weight given to easy, moderate, and hard examples; LiNC estimates how much to trust each label and corrects likely errors; and LiBaGS selects synthetic samples that fill useful gaps near a model’s decision boundary. Related work examines fairness and missingness in time-series data.

I work across image, tabular, time-series, text, and multimodal data. Projects with SickKids and UHN KITE have included pediatric cancer surveillance, renal ultrasound, and automated pain assessment. Other current work explores large language models, medical vision-language models, agentic healthcare systems, AI literacy, and hospital AI governance.
