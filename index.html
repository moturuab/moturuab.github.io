About

I study how AI systems behave when the data are messy, multimodal, and consequential.

My research spans sample difficulty, noisy supervision, fairness, missingness, synthetic data, medical imaging, automated pain monitoring, VLM evaluation, and agentic AI. The common thread is reliability: how to build models that remain useful when labels are imperfect, examples are rare, and evaluation needs to match the real task.

Health AI has been my main proving ground because it forces strong habits: careful data handling, domain expertise, safety-minded evaluation, and clear communication. Those habits are directly relevant to advanced AI teams in finance, large technology companies, research labs, and early-stage startups.
